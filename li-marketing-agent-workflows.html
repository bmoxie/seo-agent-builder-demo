Web Marketing Agent Workflows — How Work Actually Flows

Operating cycle and substrates: Master Flow. Below: agent mapping by segment of the loop, then five example diagrams (slices from trigger to artifact).

Agents on the loop

The eight agent buckets on Architecture describe what exists. Here is how those agents map onto the Sense→Learn loop (same loop shown on Master Flow).

Sense→ Decide→ Produce→ Orchestrate→ Verify→ Learn

Sense

Continuously ingest signals from every relevant surface.

External signals

  • Prompt Opportunity Agent
  • Field News & Macro Impact
  • AEO Defense, Competitive Watch & Profound Anomalies
  • SERP, AI Overview & Rich-Result Tracking
  • Persona, ICP & VoC Alignment
  • Prompt Family Health: Answers, Drift & Variance
  • Brand/Citation Gap & Competitor Sources
  • Prompt Volume, Trend & Sentiment
  • Query Migration & Search Intent Shift
  • Striking Distance, Losses & New Rank Opportunities
  • Algorithm Impact, Authority & Branded Split
  • Backlink Velocity & Quality
  • Content Gap & FAQ Cannibalization

Owned-surface signals

  • Owned Surface Change Detector
  • New Page Detector
  • Page Removal & Decay Detector
  • Cross-Property Coverage Monitor
  • Template-Level Change Detector
  • Internal Publishing Activity Stream
  • Content Freshness Heatmap
  • GSC Coverage & Indexation Agent
  • Content Inventory & Decay

Earned signals

  • Earned Signal Agent
  • Coverage & Citation Monitor
  • Community Listening Inputs

Decide

Rank, prioritize, and route signals into action queues.

  • Content Gap & FAQ Cannibalization
  • Earned Whitespace Mapper
  • Cross-Ecosystem Alignment
  • Product Expert Gut Check
  • Editorial Calendar Synth
  • Roadmap Builder
  • ROI by Initiative

This is where the Decision Agent meta-pattern lives. Its job is “here are the things worth doing this week and why,” not “here are 500 issues.” It pulls from every Sense agent, applies business logic, and produces a ranked action queue.

Generate the actual work artifacts.

Content production

  • SEO Content Engine
  • Owned Surface Refresh
  • Repurposing Agent
  • Localization Engine
  • Agency Brief + QA
  • LOB Lead Review Packet

Technical production

  • Schema Generation + Validation
  • Internal Linking Auto
  • On-Page + Decay Agent
  • Health Agent (Ahrefs → AEM)
  • 404 / Redirect Janitor
  • Image & Alt-Text Audit
  • Migration QA
  • Insight-Proof at Scale

Earned production

  • Citation-Worthy Asset Generator
  • Third-Party Co-Mention Agent
  • Publisher Briefs
  • Backlink Outreach Agent
  • Earned Co-Creation Agent
  • Awards & Speaking Opportunity Agent
  • Analyst Visibility Agent

Internal partner production

  • Thought Leadership Engine
  • Newsletter Builder
  • AEO Guidance for Social
  • Exec Thought Leadership Inputs
  • SME Prompt Pack Generator
  • Webinar / Event Promotion
  • Airtable Handoff Packets

Reporting production

  • Auto Reporting + Narrative
  • BU Stakeholder Readout
  • PPT / GSlides Builder
  • FAQ Reporting Tool
  • Analytics Visualization
  • MLT Update Builder
  • Multi-Source Pulls

Orchestrate

Route the right artifact to the right owner in the right format at the right time.

  • Ownership Registry
  • Agency Takeover Workflow Agent
  • Launch Readiness
  • Technical SEO Engine (as router)
  • Recurring Q&A Agent
  • Doc Retrieval Agent
  • Song Sheet Optimizer
  • Meeting Prep & Recap
  • Status Roll-Up

This is where the Orchestration Agent meta-pattern lives. It enforces who-owns-what, what the approval path is, what can be auto-ticketed, and what needs review. Every Produce artifact passes through Orchestrate before reaching a human.

Verify

Close the loop: confirm work shipped correctly and did not break anything.

  • Migration QA
  • Tech SEO Health Monitor
  • Conversion Path & Funnel Audit
  • Brand & Legal Compliance Sweep
  • GSC Coverage & Indexation Agent (as verifier)
  • Owned Surface Change Detector (as verifier — did the change we asked for go live?)
  • Insight-Proof at Scale
  • Backlink Audit / Disavow

Verify is what many SEO programs skip, which is why “recommendation theater” is a common failure mode. Every Produce artifact gets a corresponding Verify check tied to acceptance criteria.

Learn

Watch outcomes, update prioritization, and improve future production.

  • Experiment Agent
  • Revenue Attribution & Influence
  • Paid / Organic Sync
  • Tool ROI / Vendor Audit
  • Workflow Auditor
  • Decision Memory
  • Capacity & Throughput Tracker
  • Onboarding & SOP Agent (codifies learnings into reusable skills)

Learnings flow back into the Marketing Brain, so every future Sense, Decide, and Produce cycle gets smarter without humans re-teaching the system from scratch.

Examples below: each workflow diagram is one slice through the loop (Sense → Learn). They use the same scaffold as Architecture and Master Flow.

Workflow 1 — From AEO Visibility Drop to Published Refresh

A nightly Profound pull detects citation loss, the Refresh Agent drafts a brief, the LOB lead approves, the agency executes, and AEM ships the update.

Workflow 2 — Launch Readiness + Social Enablement Handoff

A launch is added to the Airtable calendar. A webhook fires the Launch Readiness Agent, which runs the right checklist and returns AEO guidance plus handoff notes for the social team (not channel execution ownership).

Workflow 3 — Weekly BU Readout + Deck Builder

A scheduled job pulls fresh metrics from every source, the reporting and narrative agents synthesize the BU story, and a readout plus deck land in Outlook Monday morning.

Workflow 4 — Earned Media Pitch & Outreach

A new product announcement triggers a press release, pitch angles, target journalist list, and personalized outreach drafts ready for the team to send.

Workflow 5 — Recurring Stakeholder Q&A in Teams

A stakeholder asks a question in Teams. The recurring Q&A agent pulls from the Brain and data lake, governance checks the answer, and a sourced reply lands back in chat.

How to read these: each diagram shows one realistic example of how a request enters the system, gets processed by an agent, consults the Brain, calls the right MCPs and data sources, and produces an artifact that lands in Teams, Outlook, SharePoint, Airtable, or AEM. Solid arrows show the main flow. Dashed arrows show governance checks happening alongside.

Priority dots on selected steps: green=P1 · orange=P2 · yellow=P3 · red=P4 · slate=other.

The point: the team isn't doing every task by hand anymore — they're supervising five (or fifty) flows like these, intervening only on exceptions and high-stakes approvals.
